# BSR Contract Chatbot

## Value Proposition for a Stunt Union
BSR Contract Chatbot is a contract-grounded assistant for stunt performers and union representatives. It helps users ask plain-English questions about rates, overtime, notice periods, holiday, travel, definitions, and obligations, then returns answers only from approved contract documents.

This is the core value: faster access to contract intelligence without losing trust, accuracy, or scope control.

## The Problem It Solves
Union teams and members work across multiple agreements, each with different rules and wording. Important answers are often buried in long PDFs, summary sheets, or rate cards. That creates four recurring problems:

- routine questions take too long to answer
- answers can vary depending on who responds
- newer staff and members may struggle to find the right clause
- confidence drops when people are unsure which agreement applies

For a stunt union, that means avoidable admin load, slower member support, and higher risk of inconsistent guidance.

## What the App Does
The app gives the user a simple chat interface. They choose the relevant contract, ask a question in natural language, and receive a concise answer based on the selected document set.

Current contract scopes include:

- Pact Cinema
- Pact TV & SVoD
- BBC TV
- ITV TV
- Commercial
- MoCap

The system is designed to stay inside that chosen scope. It does not mix answers across contracts, and it does not answer from general internet knowledge.

## Why This Is Valuable to the Union

### Faster Member Support
Staff can answer common contract questions in seconds rather than manually searching documents.

### More Consistent Answers
Because the assistant is tied to approved source documents, it reduces variation between different responders.

### Better Access to Union Knowledge
Members and representatives can get clause-level answers without needing to know exactly where the wording lives.

### Scales Without Adding Headcount
The app can handle repetitive first-pass questions, freeing staff to focus on disputes, negotiation, and case-specific judgement.

### Stronger Trust Than a Generic AI Tool
This is not a general chatbot. It is built to answer from contract evidence only, or refuse if the answer is not explicit.

## Key Differentiators

### Contract-Scoped by Design
The user selects the agreement first. The system then filters retrieval and response generation to that scope.

### Document-Grounded Answers
The app is built to quote or closely cite the wording it finds, rather than inventing a broad summary.

### Refusal Instead of Guessing
If the answer is not supported by the documents, the system is designed to say so. That is a major trust feature for union use.

### Useful for Real Enquiries
The product is well suited to questions such as:

- "What is the overtime rate under Pact TV & SVoD?"
- "What clause sets the termination notice period?"
- "Does the MoCap agreement define travel time?"
- "What does the contract say about holiday pay?"

## Suggested Positioning
The strongest sales message is:

**A trusted contract assistant for stunt performers and union staff that answers from the union's own agreements, not from guesswork.**

You can also position it as:

- a member self-service tool for routine contract questions
- a staff support tool for faster first-line responses
- a contract navigation tool for training and onboarding
- a foundation for a broader digital knowledge service

## Pilot Value
This is a strong pilot candidate because the problem is clear, the use cases are repetitive, and the value is easy to measure.

A union pilot could measure:

- reduced time to answer common enquiries
- improved consistency of responses
- higher confidence in finding the right clause
- lower administrative burden for routine contract lookups

## Important Framing
This should be presented as a decision-support and access tool, not a replacement for union judgement, casework, or legal advice. Its role is to surface the right contractual wording quickly and reliably so union professionals and members can act with better information.
